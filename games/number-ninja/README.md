## Overview
Number Ninja is a highly gamified math game that adds RPG-like elements to math practice. Players build combos for score multipliers, earn achievements, and enjoy particle effects and visual rewards while mastering multiplication, addition, and subtraction.

## Learning Objectives

### Primary Skills
- **Multiplication Mastery**: Fluent recall of tables 2-12
- **Mental Calculation**: Quick addition and subtraction skills
- **Sustained Focus**: Maintain concentration through engaging gameplay

### Secondary Skills
- **Strategic Thinking**: Balance speed with accuracy to maintain combos
- **Goal Setting**: Work toward achievements and high scores
- **Persistence**: Learn from mistakes (combo resets) and continue
- **Self-Motivation**: Intrinsic rewards through gamification

## Game Mechanics

### Core Features

#### Combo System
- **Build Combos**: Each correct answer increases your combo counter
- **Multipliers**: Higher combos = higher score multipliers
  - 1-2 correct: 1x multiplier
  - 3-4 correct: 1.5x multiplier (Good!)
  - 5-9 correct: 2x multiplier (Great!)
  - 10+ correct: 3x multiplier (UNSTOPPABLE!)
- **Combo Reset**: Wrong answers reset combo to 0
- **Strategy**: Accuracy over speed maintains combos

#### Scoring System
- Base score: 100 points per correct answer
- Multiplied by current combo multiplier
- Example: Answer #5 in a streak = 100 Ã— 2x = 200 points
- Total score accumulates throughout the round

#### Visual Effects

- **Ninja Stars**: Rotating answer buttons with hover effects

### Difficulty Levels

#### Apprentice (Easy)
- Multiplication: Tables 2-6
- Addition/Subtraction: 2-digit numbers
- Perfect for beginners building confidence

#### Ninja (Normal)
- Multiplication: Tables 2-10
- Addition/Subtraction: 2-3 digit numbers
- Balanced challenge for intermediate students

#### Master (Hard)
- Multiplication: All tables 2-12
- Addition/Subtraction: 3-digit numbers
- Emphasis on difficult tables (7, 9, 11, 12)
- For advanced students seeking mastery

### Game Rules
1. **20 Questions**: 16 multiplication (80%), 4 addition/subtraction (20%)
2. **2-Minute Timer**: Complete before time expires
3. **Sequential Gameplay**: Questions appear one at a time
4. **Combo Tracking**: Visible combo counter with level indicators
5. **Score Accumulation**: Points build throughout the round
6. **Leaderboard**: 100% accuracy required, ranked by time

## How to Play

1. **Select Difficulty**: Choose Apprentice, Ninja, or Master
2. **Read Instructions**: Review combo and achievement mechanics
3. **Begin Training**: Timer starts with first question
4. **Choose Answers**: Click ninja star buttons
5. **Build Combos**: String together correct answers
6. **Watch Multipliers**: See score increase with combos
7. **Avoid Errors**: Wrong answers reset combo (but game continues)
8. **Earn Achievements**: Unlock badges for milestones
9. **View Results**: See total score, max combo, and achievements
10. **Check Leaderboard**: Compare time if you scored 100%

## Pedagogical Framework

### Self-Determination Theory
Number Ninja supports three psychological needs:

**Autonomy**
- Player chooses difficulty
- Decides speed vs. accuracy balance
- Controls own strategy

**Competence**
- Combo counter shows growing skill
- Achievements mark milestones
- Score provides concrete feedback

**Relatedness**
- Leaderboard connects to peers
- Shared achievement language
- Social comparison (healthy competition)

### Behavioral Psychology
**Variable Ratio Reinforcement**
- Achievements trigger at unpredictable times
- Creates strong engagement (like video games)
- Encourages continued practice

**Immediate Reinforcement**
- Instant score feedback
- Visual rewards (particles, animations)
- Reduces delay between action and consequence

**Progressive Difficulty**
- Three levels scaffold learning
- Players advance when ready
- Prevents frustration and boredom

## Strategy Guide

### Maximizing Score
1. **Prioritize Accuracy**: Wrong answers cost more than slow answers
2. **Build Early Combos**: Higher multipliers compound over time
3. **Stay Calm**: Anxiety increases errors
4. **Know Weak Facts**: Practice difficult tables separately
5. **Read Carefully**: Rushing causes careless mistakes

### Earning Achievements
- **First Blood**: Focus on first question
- **Triple Threat**: Three correct in a row
- **Unstoppable**: 10-combo requires perfect run
- **Flawless Start**: All 10 correct from beginning (hardest!)

### Combo Maintenance
- Take an extra second to verify your answer
- Use mnemonic strategies for tough facts
- Breathe between questions

## Common Student Questions

**Q: What if I lose my combo?**
A: Don't worry! Keep going. Even without combos, you can still get 100% accuracy for the leaderboard.

**Q: Is a high score or high combo more important?**
A: For the leaderboard, only time and 100% accuracy matter. Score and combo are for fun and motivation!

**Q: Can I replay to earn achievements again?**
A: Achievements unlock once per session. Start a new round to try earning different ones!

**Q: What's the highest possible score?**
A: With a perfect 20-combo and 3x multiplier for all 20 questions: approximately 4,500 points!
